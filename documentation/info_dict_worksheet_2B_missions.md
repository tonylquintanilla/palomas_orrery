## Apollo 11 S-IVB

**Apollo 11 mission facts**
1. Splashdown: July 24, 16:50:35 UTC
2. Splashdown location: Pacific Ocean, 2660 km east of Wake Island,
   280 km south of Johnston Atoll, 24 km from recovery ship USS Hornet
3. Armstrong's exact words on stepping onto the Moon:
   "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."
   NOTE: Armstrong later said he intended "one small step for *a* man."
   What does the official NASA transcript record?
4. Time on lunar surface: ~21.5 hours
5. Lunar orbit insertion: July 19, 17:21:50 UTC
6. Translunar injection: July 16, 16:22:13 UTC

## Pioneer 10

**Pioneer 10**
1. First spacecraft to travel through the asteroid belt and make
   direct observations of it
2. First mission sent to the outer solar system
3. First spacecraft to investigate Jupiter directly
4. Closest approach to Jupiter: December 4, 1973, at ~2.8 Jovian
   radii (~200,000 km)
5. Last fully successful acquisition of signal: March 3, 2002

## JUICE (Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer)

**JUICE**
1. Launched April 14, 2023 at 12:14 UTC from French Guiana (ELA-3)
   on an Ariane 5
2. Planned Jupiter arrival: July 2031
3. Will enter orbit around Ganymede: December 2034
4. Nominal mission duration: three and a half years
5. Four gravity assist flybys of Venus and Earth before Jupiter
6. Will pass through the asteroid Main Belt twice
7. First Europa flyby: July 2032

## Juno

**Juno**
1. Launched: August 5, 2011
2. Earth flyby (gravity assist): October 9, 2013
3. Jupiter orbit insertion: July 5, 2016
4. First Ganymede flyby: June 7, 2021
5. Extended mission start: August 1, 2021
6. First Europa flyby: September 29, 2022
7. First Io flyby: December 30, 2023
8. Second Io flyby: February 3, 2024
9. End of recorded orbit data: February 15, 2025
10. End of mission: September 2025 by deliberate Jupiter impact
11. First spacecraft to orbit Jupiter from pole to pole
12. First spacecraft to operate on solar power at Jupiter's distance
    from the Sun
13. Jupiter's magnetic field is the strongest in the solar system,
    generated by a layer of metallic hydrogen deep within the planet
