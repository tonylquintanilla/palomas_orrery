## Betelgeuse (alf Ori)
1. Red supergiant -- if placed at center of solar system, surface extends beyond Jupiter's orbit
2. Surface temperature ~3,500 K
3. Luminosity > 100,000 times the Sun
4. 2019-2020 "Great Dimming" caused by a large dust cloud ejection (not imminent supernova)
5. Expected to explode as supernova within ~100,000 years

## Rigel (bet Ori)
1. Blue supergiant -- ~17 times more massive than the Sun
2. ~66,000 times more luminous than the Sun
3. Surface temperature ~11,000 K
4. Seventh brightest star in the night sky
5. Multiple star system (Rigel A + Rigel B binary + fourth distant star)

## Bellatrix (gam Ori)
1. Third brightest star in Orion, 27th brightest in entire night sky
2. Spectral type B2 III (blue giant)
3. Surface temperature ~21,500 K
4. Luminosity ~6,400 times the Sun
5. Expected to end life as a massive white dwarf

## Mintaka (del Ori)
1. Multiple star system of six or more stars
2. Main component is triple system (Aa1, Aa2, Ab)
3. Aa1 and Aa2 form an eclipsing binary
4. Spectral type O9.5 II (hot blue giants)
5. Distance ~900 light-years

## Alnilam (eps Ori)
1. Fourth brightest in Orion, 29th brightest in the night sky
2. Spectral type B0 Ia (blue supergiant)
3. Distance ~2,000 light-years
4. Luminosity ~375,000 times the Sun
5. Surrounded by reflection nebula NGC 1990

## Alnitak (zet Ori)
1. Triple star system (Alnitak Aa, Ab, B)
2. Primary (Aa) spectral type O9.7 Ibe (blue supergiant)
3. Surrounded by Flame Nebula NGC 2024
4. Some evidence Aa and Ab may form an eclipsing binary

## Fomalhaut (alf PsA)
1. Age ~440 million years
2. Distance ~25 light-years, 18th brightest star overall
3. Spectral type A3V
4. Fomalhaut b: first directly imaged exoplanet (2008); later reinterpreted as possible dust cloud
5. Fomalhaut c: second exoplanet discovered 2012

## Naos (zet Pup)
1. Apparent magnitude 2.25, brightest in Puppis
2. Mass ~22.5 times the Sun
3. Surface temperature ~42,000 K (O-type)
4. Runaway star -- ejected when binary companion went supernova
5. Rotational velocity ~220 km/s

## Antares (alf Sco)
1. If placed at center of solar system, surface extends beyond Mars's orbit
2. Surface temperature ~3,400 K (red supergiant)
3. Luminosity > 10,000 times the Sun
4. 15th brightest star in the night sky
5. Binary system (Antares A + Antares B)

## Aldebaran (alf Tau)
1. Distance ~65 light-years
2. Mass ~1.7 times the Sun
3. Radius ~44 times the Sun (extends almost to Mercury's orbit if centered)
4. Luminosity > 400 times the Sun
5. Temperature ~3,900 K (orange giant)
6. Binary system (Aldebaran A + red dwarf Aldebaran B)
