# The Viking Greenland Story: Climate & Collapse

**Tony asked:** "What eliminated the Viking settlement in Greenland?"

**Answer:** The Little Ice Age (1300-1850 CE) - a perfect case study of climate impact on civilization.

---

## The Full Story

### Phase 1: Expansion (985-1200 CE)
**Climate:** Medieval Warm Period  
**Temperature:** ~0.3°C warmer than 1850-1900 baseline

**What happened:**
- 985 CE: Erik the Red exiled from Iceland, discovers Greenland
- Names it "Greenland" for marketing (some debate if it was greener)
- During MWP: Southern Greenland could support livestock farming
- Norse established two settlements:
  - **Eastern Settlement**: ~4,000 people at peak
  - **Western Settlement**: ~1,000 people at peak
- Grew hay for livestock, raised cattle/sheep/goats
- Exported walrus ivory, furs to Europe
- Built churches (ruins still stand today)

**Key point:** Marginal farming was JUST BARELY possible during the warmth.

---

### Phase 2: Cooling (1200-1350 CE)
**Climate:** Transition from MWP to Little Ice Age  
**Temperature:** Starting to drop ~0.5-1°C

**What happened:**
- Growing season getting shorter (weeks matter at that latitude!)
- Harder to grow enough hay for winter livestock feed
- Sea ice increasing → trade routes to Europe disrupted
- Inuit peoples expanding southward (better adapted to cold)
- 1261: Greenland becomes Norwegian territory (seeking support)
- 1279: Western Settlement last mentioned in records
- ~1350: Western Settlement completely abandoned

**Key point:** As climate cooled, the Norse margin of survival shrank.

---

### Phase 3: Collapse (1350-1500 CE)
**Climate:** Little Ice Age in full swing  
**Temperature:** ~1°C cooler than Medieval Warm Period

**What happened:**
- 1350s: Black Death in Europe → reduced trade demand
- Sea ice blocks ships more frequently
- Last recorded ship from Europe: 1410 CE
- 1408: Last recorded church wedding in Eastern Settlement
- ~1450: Eastern Settlement abandoned
- 1540s: Search expedition finds empty settlements
- Bodies found, but mystery remains about final years

**Theories on final collapse:**
- Gradual emigration back to Iceland
- Disease (plague, or others)
- Conflicts with Inuit (unlikely - little evidence)
- Simply starved when couldn't feed livestock
- Probably combination of isolation + cold + reduced population

**Key point:** Climate didn't kill them directly, but made survival impossible.

---

## Why They Failed (Inuit Survived)

### Norse Strategy (Failed):
- Livestock farming requiring hay
- European-style diet (meat, dairy)
- Timber buildings (deforested their area)
- Heavy reliance on trade with Europe
- Refused to adopt Inuit technologies
- Cultural rigidity ("We are Norse, not natives")

### Inuit Strategy (Succeeded):
- Hunting/fishing based economy
- Seal, whale, caribou, fish
- Mobile lifestyle, skin tents
- Stone/sod houses (better insulated)
- Kayaks, umiaks (for sea ice hunting)
- Adapted technology to environment
- No dependence on agriculture

**The irony:** The Inuit thrived in the same environment that killed the Norse colony. Culture matters as much as climate!

## The Ukrainian Connection (Tony's Second Insight)

You also mentioned Ukrainian pastoral peoples affected by Little Ice Age. You're absolutely right!

### Eurasian Steppe Cultures During LIA:

**Who:** Cumans, Kipchaks, various Turkic/Mongolic pastoral groups

**What happened:**
- Steppe grasslands less productive in cold
- Shorter grazing seasons
- Horses (crucial for pastoral lifestyle) harder to maintain
- Pushed south toward agricultural regions
- Increased conflicts over resources
- Many groups forced to settle/merge with agricultural societies
- Mongol Empire expansion partly enabled by trying to escape cold

**Broader impacts:**
- Golden Horde weakened (1400s)
- Ottoman expansion into Eastern Europe
- Russian expansion into steppe (filling vacuum)
- Major demographic changes across Central Asia

**Pattern:** Cold = stress on pastoralism = migration/conflict

## The Lessons

### For Climate Science:
1. **Natural variability exists** - MWP and LIA happened without human forcing
2. **But scale matters** - Those swings were ±0.5°C over centuries
3. **Modern change is different** - +1.28°C in 175 years (and accelerating)
4. **Regional vs global** - MWP/LIA were mostly North Atlantic/Northern Hemisphere
5. **Current warming is global** - No place on Earth unaffected

### For Human History:
1. **Climate shapes civilization** - Vikings couldn't farm in cold
2. **Adaptation matters** - Inuit thrived, Norse failed (cultural factors)
3. **Tipping points exist** - Small climate shifts = huge human impacts
4. **Trade/isolation matters** - Norse survived while connected to Europe
5. **Margin of survival** - Greenland was always marginal, slight cooling = failure

### For Today:
1. **We're pushing faster than natural** - Ecosystems/societies can't adapt
2. **Global interconnected** - Can't just abandon like Vikings did
3. **Adaptation crucial** - But limits exist (physics, not just culture)
4. **Prevention better than cure** - Easier to prevent than adapt
5. **Indigenous knowledge matters** - Traditional adaptations relevant
