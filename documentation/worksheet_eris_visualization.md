## Group A: Eris Core (line 37)

| # | Claim | Value |
|---|-------|-------|
| A1 | Bulk density | "around 2.5 g/cm^3" |
| A2 | Density (more precise) | "2.52 +/- 0.07 g/cm^3" |
| A3 | Rock fraction | "possibly over 85%" of mass |
| A4 | Total radius | "approximately 1163 +/- 6 kilometers" |
| A5 | Core radius fraction | "roughly 50-65% of its total radius" |
| A6 | Core temperature | "possibly above 150 degC or 300 degF" |
| A7 | Central temperature model | "as high as 875 K" |
| A8 | JWST D/H ratio evidence | "detection of a moderate deuterium-to-hydrogen (D/H) ratio in methane ice" |
| A9 | "subsurface ocean at the core-mantle boundary" | Confirm this is current speculation |

Note on A4: Eris's radius has been measured by stellar occultation
(Sicardy et al. 2011) as 1163 +/- 6 km. Confirm this is still the
accepted value.

Note on A7: The 875 K claim needs a specific paper citation.

Note on A8: The JWST methane D/H claim — confirm this is from
published JWST observations and not speculative.

Likely source: Sicardy et al. (2011), Brown & Schaller (2007),
JWST Early Release Science (if A8 is real).

## Group C: Hill Sphere (lines 401, 419)

| # | Claim | Value |
|---|-------|-------|
| C1 | Hill sphere radius | "about 8.1 million kilometers" |
| C2 | Hill sphere in AU | "0.054 astronomical units" |
| C3 | "Dysnomia orbits" within this sphere | Confirm |

Note: 8.1 million km = 0.054 AU. Check: 8,100,000 / 149,597,870.7
= 0.0541 AU. Math is consistent. But is 8.1 Mkm the correct Hill
sphere radius for Eris? This depends on Eris's semi-major axis
(~67.8 AU) and mass ratio.

Likely source: NASA Solar System Dynamics.
