## Human Impacts Comparison

### Medieval Warm Period (Good Times)
- Vikings expand to Greenland, North America
- European agriculture thrives
- Population grows
- Cultural flowering (Gothic cathedrals, universities)
- Chinese Song Dynasty prosperity

### Little Ice Age (Hard Times)
- Viking Greenland colonies fail
- Crop failures, famines
- Social unrest, wars
- Witch trials (scapegoating during stress)
- Glaciers advance in Alps
- Rivers freeze that haven't frozen since

### Modern Warming (Unprecedented)
- Faster than either MWP or LIA
- Global, not just regional
- Caused by human activity (not solar/volcanic)
- Ongoing and accelerating
- Ecosystems can't adapt fast enough

## The Irony

**Climate skeptics sometimes say:**
"But what about the Medieval Warm Period? It was warm then too!"

**The response:**
"Yes! And it was ~0.3°C warmer than 1850-1900. We're now +1.28°C. 
And it took 300 years to warm that much naturally.
We've warmed 4× more, 2× faster, and we're still going.
Also, that warming was regional (mostly North Atlantic).
This warming is truly global."

**Your visualization shows this perfectly!**
Zoom out: See the Holocene stability.
Zoom in: See the natural MWP/LIA wiggles.
Zoom more: See the modern spike breaking out.
