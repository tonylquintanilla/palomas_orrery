## Comets

**Ikeya-Seki (C/1965 S1)**
1. Formally designated C/1965 S1 (code says C/1965 S1-A -- is the
   -A suffix correct for the main fragment?)
2. One of the brightest comets of the 20th century
3. Member of the Kreutz sungrazer family
4. Discovered September 18, 1965 by Kaoru Ikeya and Tsutomu Seki
   independently
5. Perihelion: October 21, 1965
6. Passed ~450,000 km (280,000 mi) above the Sun's surface
7. Nucleus fragmented into at least three pieces at perihelion
8. Orbital period estimated ~880 years

**Comet West (C/1975 V1)**
1. Formally designated C/1975 V1-A (same question -- is -A suffix correct?)
2. Discovered by Danish astronomer Richard M. West on August 10, 1975,
   at the European Southern Observatory in Chile
3. Perihelion: February 25, 1976
4. Reached apparent magnitude of -3 (comparable to Venus)
5. Nucleus broke into at least four pieces in early March 1976
6. Original (pre-perihelion) orbit eccentricity: ~0.999996
7. Post-perihelion orbit: hyperbolic (e ~1.00001), now escaping
   the solar system permanently

**Tsuchinshan-ATLAS (C/2023 A3)**
1. Discovered independently by Purple Mountain Observatory (China)
   in January 2023 and ATLAS in South Africa in February 2023
2. Originates from the Oort Cloud
3. Retrograde orbit
4. Perihelion: September 27, 2024, at 0.39 AU
5. Peak apparent magnitude: ~-4.9 in early October 2024
6. Described as "brightest comet in over 25 years" -- is this accurate?
7. Closest approach to Earth: October 12, 2024, at ~0.47 AU

**67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko**
1. Discovered in 1969 by Klim Churyumov and Svetlana Gerasimenko
2. Jupiter-family comet
3. Likely originated from the Kuiper Belt
4. Size: approximately 4.3 by 4.1 km
5. Orbital period: 6.44 years
6. Next perihelion after Aug 2015: calculated ~February 2022 --
   is this correct?
7. Rosetta: first mission ever to orbit a comet nucleus
8. Rosetta: first mission to deploy a lander onto a comet surface
9. Rosetta escorted 67P from August 2014 to September 2016
   (over two years)
10. Rosetta found 67P water vapor has a different isotopic ratio
    than Earth's oceans, suggesting comets like 67P are not the
    primary source of Earth's water
11. Rosetta launch: March 2, 2004
12. Arrival at 67P: August 6, 2014
13. Philae lander deployment: November 12, 2014
14. Mission end (deliberate impact): September 30, 2016

## Exoplanet Systems

**TOI-1338 Binary system**
1. Distance from Earth: 1,292 ly
2. Instability zone: 0.18-0.35 AU
3. Binary orbital period: 14.6 days
4. TOI-1338 A: G-type star, 1.1 solar masses
5. TOI-1338 B: M-type red dwarf, 0.3 solar masses

**TOI-1338 b**
1. Neptune-sized planet
2. ~6.9 times larger than Earth in diameter
3. Mass ~11.3 Earth masses
4. Discovered 2020 via TESS transit method
5. Found by Wolf Cukier, a 17-year-old high school intern at NASA
   (Gemini confirmed this in Round 1 -- just needs source citation
   for the code comment)
6. Orbital period: ~95.2 days
7. Semi-major axis: 0.461 AU from barycenter

**TOI-1338 c**
1. Jupiter-mass gas giant
2. Mass ~75.4 Earth masses
3. Discovered 2023 using radial velocity method
4. Described as first circumbinary planet detected by RV alone --
   is this accurate?
5. Orbital period: ~215.5 days
6. Semi-major axis: 0.794 AU from barycenter

**Proxima Centauri**
1. Nearest star to the Sun
2. Spectral type M5.5V
3. Distance: 4.24 ly

**Proxima b**
1. In the habitable zone
2. Nearest known exoplanet to Earth
3. Orbital period: 11.2 days

**Proxima d**
1. Mass: 0.26 Earth masses (sub-Earth)
2. Described as "lightest RV-detected planet" -- still accurate
   as of 2026?
3. Orbital period: 5.1 days
