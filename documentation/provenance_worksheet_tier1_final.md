## GROUP B: star_notes.py

**AD Leonis / AD Leo (line 634)**
1. Also known as Gliese 388
2. Red dwarf star
3. Known for dramatic, unpredictable flares driven by magnetic activity
4. Rotation period: ~2.7 days (much faster than the Sun)
5. Estimated age: a few hundred million years old

**Vega / Alpha Lyrae (line 673)**
1. Fifth brightest star in the night sky overall
2. Second brightest in the northern celestial hemisphere
3. Distance: ~25 light-years
4. Spectral type A0V
5. Rotation period: ~12.5 hours (causes equatorial bulge)
6. Surrounded by a dust disk (debris from planetesimal collisions)

**Spica / Alpha Virginis (line 1109)**
1. Brightest star in the constellation Virgo
2. 15th brightest star in the entire night sky
3. Distance: ~250 light-years
4. Binary system -- both stars ellipsoidal due to tidal distortion
5. Classified as a Beta Cephei variable star

**Teegarden's Star (line 1177)**
1. Spectral type M7V red dwarf
2. Radius ~11% of the Sun's
3. Mass ~9% of the Sun's
4. At least 8 billion years old
5. One of the highest proper motions known
6. Discovered in 2003 despite being relatively close
7. Hosts at least two exoplanets, both potentially in the habitable zone
